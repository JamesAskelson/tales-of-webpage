Why Tales of is Cool (and why I hate bandai namco)

Amazing Coop games

Tales of games are well known for their coop systems. Many of them feature up to 6-8 characters that are all playable and selectable by more than 1 person. This is big, because the action jrpg genre is short on coop features for a big majority of games in that genre, so a franchise that dedicated itself to letting you grab your friends and embark on a 40+ hour quest was honestly unheard of. It's sad that in the most recent game, Tales of the Arise, they focused on single player and completely removed coop when it was a huge part of the franchise for most of its lifespan.

Great stories

While not all the tales of stories are incredible, some of the best ones have top notch stories. Tales of symphonia is about a journey across the world to help your friend rid the world of beings known as desians that push slavery of the majority of the world, finding out along the way all of the conspiracy behind the journey you were pushed to take in the first place and finding out what you can do to save both your friends and the world. Tales of the Abyss is an incredible tale as well, with the main character being one of the most developed characters i've ever seen in a game, going from a snobby noble child, to coping with causing the deaths of thousands due to your own huberice, and finally coming to terms with what you've done, who you are, and what you can do for the world.

Incredible and Deep Combat Systems

Tales of is known for having many systems and combo combat that changes from game to game. Symphonia started the Multi-Line Linear Motion Battle System, where you follow a line to targets where you can block, combo, or try to jump to manuever around them. Then, in abyss, they added the free run that let you go off the line you were on to avoid attacks or remanuever yourself. Many games after added things to both spice up combat and change up tactics, like vesperia's fatal strikes to finish off weak foes, graces A/B artes system to let players basically freeform their combos, and ect. More recent games have seemed to forgotten player expression and dumbed down a lot of the combat, starting with Zesteria. Since that game, every subsequent game after has felt like they don't know what they are doing, which is a shame because they had so many fun titles in their past.
